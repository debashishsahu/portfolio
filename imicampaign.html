Campaign Management System


CHALLENGE Redesign the existing multi-channel campaign management web platform to a manager-first business-driven application for IMIcampaign.


At Pramati Technologies and IMI Mobile / May-Dec 2017 (7 Months)

Overview


The product was built incrementally over the years with only client and sales team feedbacks. Therefore it had become exponentially complex and difficult to use.

My Role


UX Research: 4/8 user interviews, user testing, competitive research.
Interaction Design: end-to-end wireframing, and prototyping

Tools

Context

What are the core functions of the Campaign Management Tool?

Built for the digital enterprise, this tool is trusted with the deployment of millions of cross-channel communications. The platform's mobile-first capabilities optimize campaigns for the smartphone, with enterprises using SMS, App Push, MMS, and Email, to maximize performance throughout the customer lifecycle.

Picking apart the challenge
statement

Redesign the existing multi-channel campaign management web platform to a manager-first business-driven application for IMIcampaign.

Starting with a "HMW"

How might we improve the Campaign Creation and Campaign Management experience for the Marketing Managers?

The Problem
I am a Marketing Manager and I know what the campaign business requirements are. There is an operations team to translate the business requirements into a campaign. Due to the disjoint modus operandi there is multiple back and forth and a lot is lost in transition. Using the system is confusing and time consuming for me.

Research

Generative Research: Expert User Interview, Contextual enquiry, Participatory design.

Understand the general workflow of users and when they need access to particular pages/features. Discover how users are currently using the product.
Evaluative Research: Heuristic evaluation.

Uncover the limitations and pain points users are facing in different situations with the platform.
Hybrid Research: Design exemplars, Card sorting

Learn about any potential improvements in workflow, information architecture, or missing features.
