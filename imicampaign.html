Campaign Management System


CHALLENGE: Redesign the existing multi-channel campaign management web platform to a manager-first business-driven application for IMIcampaign.


At Pramati Technologies and IMI Mobile / May-Dec 2017 (7 Months)

Overview


The product was developed gradually over time, in response to client requests and feedback from the sales staff.

As a result, it has grown exponentially more complicated and harder to use.

Starting with a "HMW"

How might we improve the Campaign Creation and Campaign Management experience for the Marketing Managers?

The Problem

I am a Marketing Manager and I know what the campaign business requirements are. There is an operations team to translate the business requirements into a campaign. Due to the disjoint modus operandi there is multiple back and forth and a lot is lost in transition. Using the system is confusing and time consuming for me.

01

Identify Business Goals (with an expert user)

Since digital marketing campaigns as a business has multiple jargons and domain-specific understanding. We decided to explore the current system and find out how it all functions. Get to know about all the moving parts. I then created an intended functional understanding of the dashboard reporting and the campaign management section. The following are the goals identified:

Campaign Dashboard

  • Fisheye view of campaign performance across "P&Ls" and "Campaign Groups."
  • Allow for more informed decision making: Being able to see how your marketing campaign is performing in real-time allows them to make adjustments to their strategies and tactics on the fly.

Campaign Management

  • Campaign creation and management.
  • Apprehend the hierarchical structure of the Campaign Group, Campaigns, and Follow-up Campaigns.
  • Track the progress at each campaign level encapsulating all sub-campaigns associated.

User Personas

Needs and Goals 

Since we are moving from a operator-served model to a self-served model the interaction between the Marketing Managers and the Operations team is reduced. The following needs are considered from the Marketing Manager's perspective.

Dashboard:

  • Make data-driven decisions.
  • Campaign Statistics, User Engagements & ROI.
  • Top to down statistics at a granular level.
  • See information grouped by channels concerning a given date range.

Campaign Management:

  • To represent and edit campaign details in terms of Who, What, When, and Where visually.
  • Unify campaigns at the level of the users' organization and teams around a single campaign calendar. Provide a personalized experience to the customers.

Goal KPIs:

  • Reduce Campaign Deployment turn around time by 30%
  • Get Actionable insights from to pin-point problem areas.
  • Visaulize the journey of a campaign.

Designs

01

Dashboard Design

Since there was no clear understanding of the performance matrices of campaign(s) we decided to show the information as a dashboard on the landing page. We started to a very high-level block based wireframe which slowly got refined as had multiple discussions with the stakeholders.

Rationale: This low level block diagrams helped the stakeholders design with us with their input being essential in every step of the way.

Result: Improved stakeholder buy-in - from comparing dashboard templates online to understanding how data models each visualization.

02

Data Visualization Explorations

Option 1: Card for each P&L

Each Profit & Loss group had a performance chart with the tabular data present below its respective donut chart.

Rationale: This gives a high level view of the group both visually and textually.

Feedback: There is no way to see more than two P&L group together or see the conversion.

Option 2: Conversion Funnel

To illustrate conversion the next chat explored was a conversion funnel.

Rationale: This gives cannel level breakdown of the data and managers can look at "Sent" vs "Clicks" easily.

Feedback: Conversion funnel is being used to track other processes on another platform. The goal here was to have a chart that is responsive and informative for the users.

Final: Sunburst Chart

To utilize space more efficiently compared to linear (horizontal or vertical) hierarchical visualizations, the radial orientation of the sunburst chart works best and it is interactive.

Rationale:  All elements on the same level are regarded equally important, thereby eliminating the dichotomy between the elements on the periphery and center.

Since it resembles a pie chart, which most of us are familiar with; the Sunburst chart is intuitive in nature.

Feedback: The users and stakeholders loved the using a PoC version which had emulated data. We could hear them talk about the decisions they could make out of this.

04

Listing Page Adaptations

Since the platform was extremely data heavy, we had to make a few adaptations to cater to the need.

Campaign Group & Deployments
Each Campaign Group has deployments in terms of follow-ups and new campaigns. The listing has two parts: Campaign Groups with the contact size and responses. The inner table has deployment level details such as name, channel, status, etc. The deployments are the lowest level in the chain of hierarchy.
P&L → Campaigns → Deployments

Deployment Name
The deployment names could go up from 64 characters to 128 characters. To get around the problem, I came up with the idea of putting ellipses in between the name.
Example: fifteen_charac...teen_characters

05

Calendar View Improvements

Managing Information Overload

Imagine having to manage more than a 100 meetings on your daily calendar!

Failed iterations:

  • Having only a calendar view.
  • Having only a list view with dates as filters.

Solution: Finally, we decided to create a calendar view as along with a list view so that users can look at the calendar and yet see the campaigns running in the time selected.

06

Campaign Creation
Canvas

Forms → Canvas

  • Nodes: Understood the atomicity of the form and breakdown the form into free individual units called Nodes.
  • Defining Properties: Designed properties of the nodes based on what associated values can be captured and configured.
  • Canvas integrated all the nodes on the canvas by identifying the dependencies and how each is related to another in a specific flow.

Canvas Nodes
Leveraging the Sketch's "Symbol" and "Library" functionality to speed up the workflow and maintain consistency. As this was a new concept compared to the current system we decided it would be best to ideate with mid-fidelity wireframes in order to gather better feedback.

Usability Testing

We chose to conduct usability testing with 10 users in order to gain a better understanding of the dashboard experience and campaign management from the user's perspective.

To schedule the meetings with our current users, we collaborated with the Lead Product Manager and Senior Product Manager.

As the users were key people within IMImobile's existing clientele situated in a different geographic location; we conducted remote usability test sessions with users considering both the budget & the project time-line.

For some of the designs, we have to divide the users into two groups and had them complete many activities on the InVision prototype to test two design possibilities.

Key questions the Usability Tests should answer.

Adoption

Dashboard
  • What do users think about the shift in the IA along with updates in the dashboard and campaign creation processes?
  • How does the user like dashboard experience?
  • How was the navigation experience?
  • How was the filter selection design, if the users are able to use them intuitively? How was the custom filters working for them?
  • Can the user interact with the visualizations and the charts without difficulty?
  • Can the user find the list view effortlessly?

Campaign Management
  • How does user find the Campaign/Deployment creation process?
  • Are the wizards elaborate enough to follow-through? Findability of different campaign/deployment templates.
  • How was the usability of the canvas mode vs. the older form based interface (testing with two different prototypes)?
  • Where they able to relate to Who, What & When structure in the nodes while creating the campaign/deployment?

Impact

  • Is it possible for consumers to absorb information and construct campaigns more quickly?
  • How is the learnability of the new system?
  • How much time the user are generate report of a specific campaign/deployment?
  • How much time the user are taking to create new campaign & followup campaign for the newly created campaign?

Business Impact

Positive Impact

This design overhaul has positively impacted the business and entirely transformed the product to compete with other competitors like Adobe Campaign Manager and Salesforce.

Learning Curve

The learning curve is relatively flat which was proven by the usability tests.

Metrics

The overall time-taken to navigate through the product was reduced by 50%.
Time taken to create targeted campaigns was reduced by at least 30%.
Reduced the hand-off time by a massive 45%.

Key Takeaways

Designing with real data

As there were multiple changing factors it was eye-opening to design with real data and see its positive impact.

Iterating High­-Fidelity Wireframes

As the product leaders and clients were constantly reviewing designs we had to stick to wireframes of higher and usual fidelity.

Co-designing

Involving the product team with weekly whiteboarding sessions helped us design better solutions.
